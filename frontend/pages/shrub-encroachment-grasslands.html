Encroachment Mechanisms

Climate Change

Warmer temperatures and CO₂ fertilization favor woody plants over grasses

  • Elevated CO₂ enhances C₃ plant growth
  • Extended growing seasons
  • Improved drought tolerance

Fire Suppression

Reduced fire frequency allows shrubs to mature beyond vulnerable stages

  • Shrubs create shade reducing grass fuels
  • Positive feedback loop established
  • Fire behavior fundamentally changed

Overgrazing

Removal of competitive grasses creates establishment opportunities

  • Grass competition reduced
  • Soil disturbance aids seedling establishment
  • Herbivore pressure selects for unpalatable species

Soil Changes

Woody plants alter soil properties favoring further encroachment

  • Nitrogen fixation enriches soils
  • Improved water retention
  • Mycorrhizal associations provide advantages

Management Strategies

Prescribed Fire

Strategic burning to reduce woody biomass and restore grass dominance

  • Timing critical for grass recovery
  • Requires skilled fire management
  • Most cost-effective long-term

Mechanical Control

Physical removal of shrubs using heavy equipment

  • Immediate results
  • High initial cost
  • Soil disturbance concerns

Chemical Control

Targeted herbicide application for shrub control

  • Selective application possible
  • Environmental concerns
  • Requires expertise

Grazing Management

Using herbivores to control woody plant expansion

  • Sustainable approach
  • Requires animal management
  • Species-specific effectiveness
