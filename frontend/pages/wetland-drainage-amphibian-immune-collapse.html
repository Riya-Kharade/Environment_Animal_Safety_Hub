Case Studies

Real-world examples demonstrating the devastating impacts of wetland drainage on amphibian populations.

Everglades

Everglades Drainage

Florida, USA

50% wetland loss since 1900 has led to 70% decline in native frog species and emergence of chytrid fungus.

Wetland Loss: 50%
Species Decline: 70%
Bd Prevalence: 1970s
Danube Basin

Danube River Basin

Europe

Agricultural intensification has caused 60% wetland drainage, resulting in elevated Bd prevalence and ranavirus outbreaks.

Drainage: 60%
Bd Rate: 15-25%
Ranavirus: Events
Mekong Delta

Mekong Delta

Create landscape corridors and modify infrastructure for amphibian movement.

Artificial Refugia

Construct temporary wetlands during critical migration and breeding periods.
