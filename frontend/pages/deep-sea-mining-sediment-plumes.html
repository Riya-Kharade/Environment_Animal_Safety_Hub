Deep-Sea Mining Sediment Plumes

Analyzing smothering impacts on slow-growing benthic communities

Mining Operations

Deep-sea mining for valuable minerals creates massive sediment plumes

Sediment Dispersion

Particles spread over vast areas, affecting benthic habitats

Benthic Communities

Slow-growing organisms face smothering and habitat loss

Long-term Recovery

Decades to centuries needed for ecosystem restoration

Mining Methods

  • Nodule Collection: Harvesting polymetallic nodules from abyssal plains
  • SMS Mining: Extracting minerals from hydrothermal vents
  • Crust Mining: Removing cobalt-rich ferromanganese crusts from seamounts

Plume Formation

  • Resuspension: Mining activities disturb seafloor sediments
  • Discharge: Return water and tailings create near-field plumes
  • Dispersion: Currents transport sediments over vast distances

Smothering Mechanism

  • Sediment Deposition: Particles settle on benthic organisms
  • Burial Effects: Complete coverage prevents feeding and respiration
  • Suffocation: Clogging of respiratory structures

Echinoderms

Vulnerability: Medium-High

Molluscs

Vulnerability: Medium

Growth Rate: Millimeters to centimeters per year
