The Stratification Phenomenon

Thermal stratification occurs when freshwater lakes develop distinct temperature layers, creating barriers to vertical mixing. This natural process is intensifying due to climate change, leading to prolonged stratification periods that severely impact oxygen distribution and aquatic communities.

Ecosystem Consequences

Phytoplankton Community Changes

  • Increased cyanobacteria dominance
  • Reduced nutrient availability from deep waters
  • Higher risk of harmful algal blooms
  • Changes in primary production timing

Zooplankton Responses

  • Vertical migration to preferred temperature zones
  • Changes in body size and reproduction
  • Altered predator-prey relationships
  • Species composition shifts

Fish Population Impacts

  • Habitat compression for cold-water species
  • Reduced spawning success
  • Changes in migration patterns
  • Economic losses for fisheries

Benthic Community Effects

  • Oxygen stress and mortality
  • Increased internal nutrient loading
  • Loss of biodiversity
  • Changes in decomposition rates
