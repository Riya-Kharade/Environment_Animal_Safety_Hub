Threats to Bioluminescent Organisms

Pollution Impacts

Chemical Contaminants

High Impact

Heavy metals and organic pollutants disrupt enzyme function and luciferin synthesis

Dinoflagellates, copepods, fish

Plastic Pollution

High Impact

Microplastics cause physical damage and chemical toxicity

All marine luminescent species

Light Pollution

Medium Impact

Artificial lighting disrupts natural behavior and communication

Coastal and shallow-water species

Climate Change Effects

Ocean Warming

High Impact

Temperature stress reduces enzyme efficiency and metabolic rates

Tropical and temperate species

Ocean Acidification

Medium Impact

pH changes affect calcification and symbiotic relationships

Corals, mollusks, some fish

Extreme Weather

Medium Impact

Storms and heatwaves cause direct mortality and habitat damage

Coastal and intertidal species

Case Study: Caribbean Bioluminescent Bays

90% decline in luminescence
50+ species affected
$25M tourism loss

Historical bioluminescent bays in Puerto Rico and Jamaica have experienced dramatic declines due to sewage pollution, coastal development, and warming waters. The famous "milky seas" phenomenon has largely disappeared from many locations.
