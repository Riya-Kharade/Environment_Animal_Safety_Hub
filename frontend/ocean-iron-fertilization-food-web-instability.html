The Iron Hypothesis

In high-nutrient, low-chlorophyll (HNLC) ocean regions, iron availability limits phytoplankton growth despite abundant macronutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, silicon).

Historical Fertilization Experiments

IronEx I (1993)

First mesoscale iron fertilization experiment in equatorial Pacific. Demonstrated iron limitation and bloom potential.

Chlorophyll: 3x increase Duration: 2 weeks Export: Limited

SOFeX (2002)

Southern Ocean experiments testing diatom response and carbon export. First experiments south of Polar Front.

Chlorophyll: 2-3x increase Duration: 3-4 weeks Export: Variable

EIFEX (2004)

European Iron Fertilization Experiment in Northeast Atlantic. Focused on ecosystem-level responses.

Chlorophyll: 10x increase Duration: 3 weeks Export: Moderate

LOHAFEX (2009)

Last major experiment in Indian Ocean. Two patches with different iron delivery methods.

Chlorophyll: 2-4x increase Duration: 4 weeks Export: High in Patch 1
