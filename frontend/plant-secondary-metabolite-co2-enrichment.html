Mechanisms of Chemical Change

Photosynthetic Mechanisms

Carbon Assimilation Enhancement

Elevated CO‚ÇÇ increases Rubisco carboxylation efficiency, leading to enhanced photosynthetic rates and carbohydrate accumulation. This creates a carbon surplus that dilutes nitrogen-containing defensive compounds.

Stomatal Regulation

Reduced stomatal conductance improves water use efficiency but may limit nutrient uptake, creating imbalances in carbon and nitrogen allocation that affect secondary metabolite production.

Resource Allocation Shifts

Plants prioritize growth over defense under CO‚ÇÇ enrichment, diverting resources from secondary metabolite production to structural carbohydrates and increased biomass.

Biosynthetic Pathways

Phenylpropanoid Pathway

Changes in phenylalanine ammonia-lyase (PAL) activity affect flavonoid, tannin, and lignin production. CO‚ÇÇ enrichment can either stimulate or suppress these pathways depending on nitrogen availability.

Terpenoid Biosynthesis

MEP and DOXP pathways for isoprenoid production are affected by carbon flux changes. Essential oils and resin composition shift, altering herbivore deterrence and attraction cues.

Alkaloid Production

Nitrogen-limited alkaloid synthesis pathways show variable responses. Some compounds decrease due to resource limitation, while others may increase through regulatory changes.

Ecological Consequences

Herbivore Feeding Patterns

Altered plant chemistry changes host plant selection, feeding rates, and digestive efficiency. Herbivores may experience improved nutrition despite reduced defenses.

Trophic Interactions

Changes cascade through food webs, affecting predator-prey dynamics, parasitoid-host relationships, and competitive interactions among herbivore species.

Community Structure

Plant community composition shifts based on species-specific responses to CO‚ÇÇ enrichment, affecting biodiversity and ecosystem function.
